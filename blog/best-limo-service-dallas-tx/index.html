7 Things to Check Before Booking a Dallas Limo Company

A polished website is easy to build. A reliable operation is not. Before you hand over a deposit, run any Dallas-Fort Worth limo company through this seven-point checklist:

  • Licensing and permits. A legitimate operator carries the proper Texas commercial and for-hire vehicle permits. If a company can't tell you its operating authority, keep looking.
  • Commercial insurance. Personal auto insurance does not cover paid passengers. Ask for proof of commercial liability coverage — a real company provides it without hesitation.
  • Fleet age and condition. Stock photos hide a lot. Ask how old the actual vehicle is and request recent photos. You can browse our current vehicles on the fleet page so you know exactly what arrives.
  • Verified reviews. Look for a consistent track record across many riders, not three glowing reviews from last year. Read the testimonials page and cross-check third-party platforms.
  • Flat-rate vs. surge. The best companies quote a flat, all-inclusive rate. If pricing "depends on demand," you're being set up for a surprise.
  • Communication. Test it before you book — send a question and time the response. Slow or vague replies before they have your money tell you everything about the day of your ride.
  • Chauffeur background checks. Your driver should be a screened, professional chauffeur, not a gig worker who signed up last week. Ask directly.

Red Flags to Avoid

Some warning signs are subtle, but a few are dealbreakers. Walk away if you encounter any of these:

  • No written quote. A verbal "around $400" is not a price. Insist on the full cost in writing, including gratuity and any add-ons.
  • No proof of insurance. A company that dodges this question is gambling with your safety and your wallet.
  • No chauffeur standards. No uniform, no dress code, no screening process — these point to a loose operation that treats your event as just another gig.
  • Cash-only or deposit-only by app. Reputable companies offer clear, traceable payment and a real contract.
  • Bait-and-switch vehicles. If the answer to "will I get the exact car I'm booking?" is fuzzy, assume you won't.
