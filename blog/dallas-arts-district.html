Dallas Arts District: The Complete Culture Guide

By Purple Heart Limo · Mar 2026 · Dallas-Fort Worth, TX

The Dallas Arts District is the largest urban arts district in the United States. 68 acres of museums, concert halls, galleries, and architecture in the heart of downtown. If you haven't spent a full day here, you're missing one of Dallas's best-kept secrets.

Getting to the Arts District

The Arts District is walkable once you're there, but getting there and parking is notoriously difficult. Our chauffeurs drop you at the DMA entrance and return when you call.

Key Highlights

  • Dallas Museum of Art — one of the top 10 largest in the US, free admission
  • Nasher Sculpture Center — Renzo Piano building with a stunning outdoor garden
  • Crow Museum of Asian Art — free admission, deeply undervisited gem
  • AT&T Performing Arts Center — home of Dallas Opera and Texas Ballet
  • Meyerson Symphony Center — I.M. Pei masterpiece, home of the DSO
  • Flora Street Café — Stephan Pyles' crown jewel for dinner after the museum
  • Klyde Warren Park — built over a freeway, connects Arts District to Uptown
  • Booker T. Washington High School for the Performing Arts — right in the district
  • Trammell Crow Center — public sculpture garden at the base of a corporate tower
  • One Arts Plaza — luxury residential and dining in the heart of the district
