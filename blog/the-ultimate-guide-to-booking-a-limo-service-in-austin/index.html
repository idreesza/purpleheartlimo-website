The Ultimate Guide to Booking a Limo Service in Austin

Apr 6, 2026 · 8 min read · Purple Heart Limo Austin TX

This is the guide we wish existed when people first started asking us how to book a limo. It covers everything from the first Google search to the moment your ride arrives — including the questions nobody thinks to ask until something goes wrong, and the red flags that tell you to keep looking before you commit to a company.

Before You Start: Define Your Needs

The most valuable thing you can do before contacting any limo company is get clear on your requirements. This makes every conversation faster and the quote you receive more accurate. Here's what to know before you call:

  • How many people? Vehicle capacity determines your options. Be accurate — "about 8" and "definitely 12" get very different vehicle recommendations.
  • Date and time? Saturday night in April during prom season vs. a Tuesday morning airport run require completely different availability planning.
  • What type of service? Point-to-point (A to B), hourly as-directed (driver stays with you for multiple stops), or a specific event?
  • What's the occasion? The right vehicle for a corporate airport run differs from the right vehicle for prom night or a bachelorette party.
  • What's your budget? Knowing this helps you choose the right vehicle tier and avoid disappointment.

The Step-by-Step Booking Process

1

Contact and Get a Quote

Call, text, or book online. Provide your date, time, group size, pickup/drop-off locations, and occasion. At Purple Heart Limo, we respond to every inquiry within the hour during business hours, and we confirm prices clearly upfront.

2

Ask the Right Questions

Before committing to any company, ask: Is this price all-inclusive (gratuity, tolls, fuel)? Do you own your vehicles? Are drivers background-checked and drug-tested? What's your cancellation policy? What's your backup plan if a vehicle has mechanical issues? A company that hesitates on any of these is telling you something.

3

Get Written Confirmation

Any legitimate company sends written confirmation with: vehicle type and description, pickup time and location, total price (confirmed all-inclusive), driver contact information, and cancellation policy. If they won't provide written confirmation, do not book.

4

The Day Before

Save your driver's contact information in your phone. Confirm the pickup address is accessible. For airport rides, make sure your flight number is in the booking. Review your confirmation once more. This 5-minute step eliminates 90% of day-of stress.

5

Day-Of: Be Ready, Communicate Freely

Be ready slightly before your scheduled time — professional drivers arrive early. If anything changes (flight update, address change, time adjustment), text your driver directly. Communication solves nearly every potential problem before it becomes an actual one.

Red Flags That Say "Keep Looking"

  • Unable to tell you the specific vehicle that will be used for your booking
  • Reluctance to confirm pricing in writing before you book
  • No clear answer on whether gratuity is included
  • Vague answers about driver background checks ("we work with many drivers")
  • No written confirmation sent after booking
  • No 24/7 contact number in case of day-of issues
  • Recent reviews describing cancellations, no-shows, or wrong vehicles
