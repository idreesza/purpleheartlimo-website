Love Field vs. DFW — Which Airport and Why It Matters for Your Ride

Dallas has two commercial airports, and the one you choose changes your whole trip. Dallas Love Field (DAL) sits just northwest of downtown near Uptown, while DFW International is a much larger five-terminal airport halfway between Dallas and Fort Worth.

  • Airlines: Love Field is the home hub of Southwest Airlines, plus limited Delta service. DFW is dominated by American Airlines and is where nearly all international flights land.
  • Location: DAL is roughly 7 miles from downtown Dallas — often a 12–18 minute ride. DFW is 20-plus miles out and can take 30–45 minutes depending on traffic.
  • Size: DAL has one main terminal with about 20 gates, so the walk from your gate to the curb is short. DFW spreads across five separate terminals connected by a train.
  • Feel: Love Field is calmer and quicker to get in and out of, which is exactly why so many frequent flyers prefer it for short domestic hops.

The takeaway: if your destination is Uptown, downtown, the Park Cities, or North Dallas, Love Field is usually the faster, lower-stress option. If you are flying internationally or connecting on American, you will be at DFW.
