DFW to DAL: The Complete Guide to Airport Transfers in Dallas

June 6, 2026 · 9 min read · Purple Heart Limo DFW TX

Few metro areas in the country are served by two major airports the way Dallas-Fort Worth is. DFW International Airport and Dallas Love Field (DAL) sit roughly 20 miles apart, and travelers move between them — and between either airport and destinations across the metroplex — constantly. Whether you're connecting between the two, catching a Southwest flight out of Love Field after landing at DFW, or simply need a reliable ride into the city, this is your complete guide to getting from DFW to DAL in comfort. At Purple Heart Limo, a Texan-owned limousine company serving Dallas, we run this exact route every day.

Exploring Your DFW to DAL Transportation Options

There's no shortage of ways to cross from one Dallas airport to the other, but they are not created equal. Rideshare can work, but surge pricing during peak travel hours and unpredictable wait times at the DFW pickup zones make it a gamble when you have a flight to catch. Airport shuttles are economical but slow, with multiple stops and fixed schedules that rarely match your itinerary. A taxi is faster than a shuttle but offers no guarantee of vehicle quality or a professional, vetted chauffeur.

A dedicated DFW airport car service solves all three problems: a professional driver tracks your flight, meets you at the right terminal, and takes the most efficient route to Love Field without surge pricing or shared stops. For travelers landing at Love Field instead, our Dallas Love Field car service works exactly the same way in reverse. You can compare vehicle classes — from executive sedans to luxury SUVs and Sprinter vans — on our fleet page before you decide.

Best Routes from DFW to DAL Airport

The drive between DFW and Love Field is short on a map but heavily dependent on the time of day. There are two practical routes, and a good chauffeur chooses between them in real time based on live traffic.

~20 miles · 25–35 min off-peak

Route 1: TX-183 / John W. Carpenter Freeway

The most direct path heads east on TX-183 from DFW toward Love Field. It's the shortest in distance and usually fastest outside of rush hour. During the morning and evening commute, however, the 183 corridor through Irving can slow considerably.

~22 miles · 30–45 min in traffic

Route 2: TX-114 to Dallas North Tollway

When 183 is congested, looping north on TX-114 and dropping down the Dallas North Tollway often saves time despite the slightly longer distance. The tollway flows freely more reliably during peak periods, which matters when you have a boarding deadline at DAL.

The takeaway: a fixed shuttle or an unfamiliar rideshare driver follows one route regardless of conditions. A professional chauffeur who runs the DFW to DAL corridor daily adjusts to keep you on schedule — which is the whole point of booking a dedicated transfer.

Travel Tips for the DFW to DAL Journey

  • Build in a buffer. DFW is one of the largest airports in the world; getting from a far terminal to the curb takes time. For a connecting flight at Love Field, give yourself a generous cushion — your chauffeur will, too.
  • Share your flight number. With it, we track your actual arrival and adjust pickup automatically for delays or early landings, with a complimentary grace period built in.
  • Confirm the meeting point. DFW and DAL have different pickup procedures. A professional service tells you exactly where to meet your driver so there's no wandering with luggage.
  • Traveling as a group? A single Sprinter van is almost always cheaper and simpler than multiple rideshares — and everyone stays together.

DFW to DAL: Frequently Asked Questions

How far is DFW to DAL?

Dallas Fort Worth International Airport (DFW) and Dallas Love Field (DAL) are about 18 to 22 miles apart depending on the route, which is typically a 25 to 40 minute drive in normal traffic and longer during rush hour.

How much does a car service from DFW to DAL cost?

A professional car service transfer between DFW and DAL generally ranges from about $75 to $150 for a sedan or SUV, depending on vehicle class, time of day, and wait time. Purple Heart Limo quotes flat rates with no surge pricing.

Is it better to fly into DFW or DAL?

DFW International offers far more domestic and international flights, while Dallas Love Field is smaller, closer to downtown Dallas, and primarily served by Southwest Airlines. Your choice usually depends on your airline and final destination within the metroplex.
