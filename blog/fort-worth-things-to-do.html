Best Things to Do in Fort Worth — The Cowboy Capital Guide

By Purple Heart Limo · Apr 2026 · Dallas-Fort Worth, TX

Fort Worth is where Texas mythology becomes real. The Stockyards still run a cattle drive twice daily. The Kimbell Art Museum rivals anything in New York. Sundance Square buzzes on Friday nights. This city rewards visitors who actually explore it.

Fort Worth Is Worth the Trip

Fort Worth is 30 minutes west of Dallas, but most visitors only see one city or the other. That's a mistake. A day that starts in Dallas and ends at Billy Bob's Texas in the Stockyards is one of the great DFW experiences.

Key Highlights

  • Fort Worth Stockyards — daily cattle drives at 11:30am and 4pm, absolutely worth it
  • Kimbell Art Museum — free general admission, stunning Renzo Piano building
  • Sundance Square — 35 blocks of dining, entertainment, and live music
  • Fort Worth Zoo — consistently top-10 nationally, more intimate than the Dallas Zoo
  • Bass Performance Hall — world-class acoustics for concerts and opera
  • National Cowgirl Museum and Hall of Fame — genuinely fascinating
  • Amon Carter Museum of American Art — free admission, great permanent collection
  • Fort Worth Botanic Garden — 23 specialty gardens on 110 acres
  • Trinity Trails — 100+ miles of trail along the Trinity River
  • TCU Campus — beautiful grounds, home of the Horned Frogs
